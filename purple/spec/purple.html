Introduction

This article describes the planned Purple system. It serves as both documentation, helping people get a mental image of what Purple is all about, and as a guide for implementors of the Purple architecture.

This is a rather technical article, that doesn't shy away from including C declarations of important functions, and so on. The reader is assumed to be interested in the more technical/"under the hood" aspects of the Purple system, the details that make it all work, rather than being primarily focused on the "softer" parts like how it will look, what can be done with it, and so on.

The article is organized into six major sections; first a system overview with definitions of important terms. Then follows a section that talks a bit about the basic data flow mechanism that Purple is based on, and finally come one section dedicated to each of the four major architectural components that make up the system as designed.
